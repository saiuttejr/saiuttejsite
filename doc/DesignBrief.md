# Design Brief — Personal Website

**Sai Uttej Rajoju**
*Software Engineer & Product Builder · Hyderabad, India*

---

## Who I Am

I'm a backend software engineer with a product builder's sensibility. My work lives at the intersection of well-crafted systems and thoughtful product thinking — I build things that are meant to last, and I care about the quality of what's underneath as much as what's on the surface. I'm currently working at a software firm in Hyderabad while actively building and shipping my own projects on the side — a personal finance tracker, a food intelligence tool, an open-source web games collection. None of these are side projects in the casual sense. They're experiments in shipping real things with intent.

## What This Website Is For

This isn't a résumé with a domain name. I want a digital presence that communicates who I am before it lists what I've done. The person visiting should leave with a feeling — *this person takes their craft seriously* — without me having to say it outright. The work should speak quietly but clearly.

It should serve as a home base for my professional identity — somewhere I can point to when a recruiter, collaborator, or curious person wants to understand what I'm about. It doesn't need to be exhaustive. It needs to be precise.

## The Feeling I'm After

Premium and refined. Not a "developer portfolio." Think closer to a high-end editorial or a well-designed magazine spread — the kind of site that makes you slow down rather than scroll through. I want smooth, purposeful motion. Confidence without loudness. The aesthetic of someone who has taste, not someone who has templates.

Caliber should be felt, not announced. No hero sections screaming titles. No bullet-point skill lists. No *"I am passionate about building scalable solutions."* The design itself should do the work that lesser sites leave to copy.

## Creative Direction

I'm giving you full creative freedom on how this is realized. I have a direction in mind — not a design. The balance I care about is this: equal weight between the engineer and the builder. I write clean, structured code. I also think about users, products, and strategy. Both sides of that should come through in the experience of the site itself, not just in what it says.

Treat this as a long-form brand identity exercise as much as a website. What does the typography say about how I think? What does the pacing of the scroll say about how I work? Those questions matter more to me than which sections to include.

## What I Want to Avoid

Anything that reads as "techie" — dark terminals, neon accents, Matrix-adjacent aesthetics, floating code snippets used decoratively. No performative busyness. No achievement lists that read like a LinkedIn profile. Nothing that suggests I'm trying to impress rather than simply show.
